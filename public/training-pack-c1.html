C1 · Kaja chest-framed testimonial

A deep, step-by-step training module for the first and most important shoot of the day: a quiet, emotionally grounded handheld monologue. This pack teaches every decision — framing, eye-line, audio, light, delivery tone — so both of you can walk onto set knowing exactly what "good" looks like and why.

Priority 1 Dialogue-led 1 camera Handheld 20 to 30s final ~45 min on set Difficulty: easy to medium

Learning goals

  • Hold a phone steady at chest height for a 30-second take without visible drift
  • Frame a subject chest-up with eyes on the top third and understand why it works
  • Route a DJI Mic 2 to an iPhone and run a clean audio test
  • Build a single-source soft-key lighting setup that looks like daylight
  • Coach a talent out of "performance mode" and into delivery mode

Pre-requisites

  • Read Training Companion Parts 1, 2, and 3 (angles, lighting, audio)
  • Open the shoot pack sheet for C1 so you can cross-reference the grid
  • Pass Module 1 of the training course (framing basics)
  • Fully charge both iPhones the night before

What you'll deliver

  • 3 clean dead-on handheld takes of the full monologue, chest-framed
  • 2 to 3 takes of the same script at a 3/4 side angle
  • 4 silent cut-away beats (window, fridge, microwave, eating)
  • Camera-original files, no in-phone edits, labelled C1-A and C1-B

01 · Concept

What we're making and why it works

The selling point of this ad is not the meal. It is Kaja's relief.

This is a quiet handheld monologue. Kaja speaks directly to camera, chest-framed, with one piece of light and no music. The emotional payoff is a single line: "I haven't thought about dinner in six weeks." Everything in the frame is in service of that line landing.

The reason this format scores well in the format brief is counter-intuitive: lower production feels more real, and real feels more trustworthy. The highest-performing Simmer and Megan Petite Coach ads this quarter were filmed in kitchens, handheld, with a single collar mic, by one person. Polish is the enemy here. If the lighting looks like a commercial, the line stops working.

Plain-english summaryKaja stands in the kitchen, James holds the phone at his chest, and Kaja tells a real story in her own voice. No music, no tripod, no performance. It feels like she's telling a friend. That's the whole job.

Reference winners this pattern came from

  • Simmer ad 1 — quiet handheld monologue, chest-framed, no music. Scored for intimacy over polish.
  • Megan Petite Coach ad 3 — relief as the emotional payoff. Same structure, different voice.
  • Both ads use daylight only or a single soft source. Neither uses a tripod. Neither uses music until the final text card.

03 · Framing and camera

Framing, settings, and crop

Chest-framed means collarbone at the bottom, a small headroom at the top. Eyes on the top third.

Chest-framed, dead-on
Take A. James stands directly in front of Kaja, phone at his own chest height. Kaja's eyes fall on the top third line. Small headroom above.
3/4 angle, hip height
Take B. James moves to Kaja's right (her 4 o'clock), phone dropped to hip height, angled up slightly. Wider framing.

Camera settings (iPhone 17 Pro Max)

SettingValueWhy
ModeVideoObvious, but double-check. Easy to accidentally record stills.
Resolution4KLets the editor crop into a 1:1 or square version without quality loss.
Frame rate30fpsMatches the look of organic social. 60fps feels too "ad-like" for this format.

Exposure lockLong-press to lock on Kaja's face before recordingStops the phone from exposure-drifting mid-take if she moves near a window.
04 · Lighting

Lighting setup, step by step

One source. Soft. Daylight-coloured. Treat the Amaran as if it's a window.

Top-down lighting plan
Amaran 200x + Dome III camera-left at 45°. Window behind Kaja as a practical backlight. James stands in front of Kaja with the phone.

Step by step

  1. Find the window. Position Kaja so a window is roughly behind her or to her back-left. Daylight is doing half the work here.
  2. Place the Amaran 200x on its stand, camera-left, 1.5m from Kaja, at head height. Point it at her face.
  3. Fit the Dome III softbox over the Amaran. Front diffusion panel clicked on. The broad face of the softbox must point at Kaja.
  4. Set colour temperature to 5600K (daylight). This matches the window behind her so the two sources don't fight.
  5. Set brightness around 20% to start. It should feel like a soft window wash on her face, not a studio key. If you can see a hard shadow on her nose, raise the light or move it further away.
  6. Walk around Kaja with the phone and check: no hot spot on her forehead, no harsh shadow under her chin, catch-lights visible in her eyes. If not, nudge the Amaran up or down by 10cm at a time.
  7. Kill the overhead kitchen lights. They'll colour-clash with the daylight and flatten the face. Daylight plus the one Amaran is the whole setup.
Rule of thumbIf you can tell there's a light on in the room, the light is too bright. This shot is daylight-first, Amaran-second, everything else off.
05 · Audio

Audio setup and test

Audio wins or loses this ad. Bad picture is forgivable. Bad audio is an instant scroll.

  1. Unpack the DJI Mic 2 case. You need 1 TX (transmitter, the clip-on box with the bead mic) and the RX (receiver, the small round disc with USB-C).
  2. Power both on. Long-press the power button on each until the screen lights up. Pair should be automatic (they were paired on the test day). Watch the RX screen show a green "linked" icon.
  3. Plug the RX into the iPhone 17 Pro Max. It goes directly into the USB-C port, no adapter. The iPhone will pop a permission prompt the first time, allow it.
  4. Open the iPhone Camera app. Top-right, tap the audio icon if visible. Otherwise go to Settings → Camera → Record Audio and confirm external input is selected.
  5. Clip the TX to Kaja. Under her collar, mic bead pointing up toward her mouth, about a fist's width below her chin. Cable tucked behind the shirt. TX itself in a back pocket or clipped to waistband, not dangling.
  6. Audio test. James starts a recording on the iPhone. Kaja counts to ten, normal voice, standing where she'll stand for the take. James stops, plays back on headphones (AirPods fine). Listen for: clear voice, no crackle, no clothing rustle, no hum from the fridge.
  7. If you hear clothing rustle, the clip is catching a zip or necklace. Re-clip onto fabric only. Test again.
  8. If you hear hum, the fridge or a fan is close. Turn them off for the take.
  9. Monitor during takes. James keeps one AirPod in during the actual takes. If something goes wrong mid-take you can catch it live, not in the edit.
Back-up planIf the DJI Mic 2 fails or won't pair, fall back to the iPhone's onboard mic. Move James within 50cm of Kaja and keep the environment silent. Quality drops but the shot is still usable.
06 · Wardrobe and set

Wardrobe and set dressing

Nothing in Kaja's outfit or the kitchen should compete with her face.

Do
  • Plain muted top: cream, stone, soft navy, rust, or light grey
  • Hair the way Kaja naturally wears it on a work-from-home day
  • Minimal jewellery. Small studs at most. No clinking.
  • 4 to 6 branded containers in the fridge, label-forward but not perfectly aligned
  • One container sitting on the counter behind Kaja, soft-focus
  • A cutting board or wooden spoon visible. Lived-in kitchen cues.
Don't
  • Chefly-branded clothing on Kaja. Breaks the "real customer" feel.
  • Busy patterns or logos. They pull focus and compress badly on social.
  • Heavy make-up. Natural skin sells "real".
  • Perfectly aligned, museum-lit containers. Too commercial.
  • Competitor products or other brand logos in shot. Bin them or hide them.
  • Dishes in the sink visible. Clear the background line of sight.
07 · Script

The script, annotated

Kaja, read this once to learn the beats. Do not memorise. Say it like you'd say it to a friend.

0:00
"I used to spend Sunday nights just dreading the week." Flat delivery. Tired, not dramatic. Land on the word "dreading" but don't push it.
0:04
"Not because of work. Because of food." Tiny pause after "work". The reveal lands on "food". One-beat pause before the next line.
0:08
"What am I eating tomorrow. What do I need to buy. Have I got protein in." These three sentences speed up slightly, like you're listing the loop. No commas in the delivery, just the dot-dot-dot of tired admin.
0:13
"Every single night, the same loop." Slow back down. Small sigh before the line if it feels natural.
0:16
"Now it's just… there. Fridge. Done." (small shrug, half-smile) This is the turn. The tone shifts. The shrug is the move. Do not over-act. A small one-shoulder shrug is plenty.
0:20
"I haven't thought about dinner in six weeks." The hero line of the whole ad. Straight delivery. Eye contact with the lens. Do not smile through it.
0:24
"And I didn't realise how much space that takes up until it was gone." Soft, reflective. End the line, then hold the look for one more beat before James cuts. This is the most important beat in the ad.
0:28
Text overlay in post: "Chefly. Stop thinking about dinner." Added in CapCut. Syne Extra Bold. Cream on a near-black card. No music yet.

Behind cameraJames

09 · Timing plan

Minute-by-minute on the day

45 minutes from empty kitchen to wrap. Stick to this. Drift costs C2 later.

min 0 to 10Setup
James builds the lighting per Section 04. Kaja reads the script twice quietly, does not memorise. Put branded containers in the fridge, label-forward. Clear the counter.
min 10 to 13Audio test
Clip DJI TX to Kaja. James plugs RX into iPhone. Record a 10-count. Play back on AirPods. Fix any rustle or hum before continuing.
min 13 to 22Take A (3 passes)
James stands dead-on, phone at chest height. Kaja reads the script straight through, once. Reset, water, go again. Three times total. Between passes, James checks playback of the last pass for focus drift or audio issues.
min 22 to 30Take B (2 to 3 passes)
James moves to Kaja's 4 o'clock, drops phone to hip, angles slightly up. Kaja delivers the same script again. Two passes minimum, three if first one is shaky.
min 30 to 40Cut-aways
Shoot shots 3 to 6 in order. Each one: 2 minutes of setup and filming, 30 seconds of playback check. Move on. Don't second-guess.
min 40 to 45Review + wrap
Sit together. Scrub through the takes. Confirm: one strong take of A, one of B, four cut-aways, clean audio throughout. Label files C1-A-take1.mov and so on as you AirDrop them off the phone.
10 · Quality gates

You're done when...

Do not wrap until all of these are ticked.

C1 sign-off checklist
  • At least one complete take of the script from Take A (chest-framed, dead-on)
  • At least one complete take of the script from Take B (3/4 angle, hip height)
  • All four cut-away beats captured (window, fridge, microwave, eating)
  • Audio on the primary takes is clean on AirPod playback — no clothing rustle, no fridge hum
  • No one in the kitchen has spoken during a take except Kaja
  • Files transferred off the phone (AirDrop to laptop) and labelled C1-A-takeN / C1-B-takeN / C1-CA-N
  • Overhead kitchen lights were off for the dialogue takes
  • The hero line "I haven't thought about dinner in six weeks" has at least one take you'd happily show a stranger
11 · Common mistakes + rescues

What usually goes wrong and how to fix it fast

Kaja performs instead of delivers

You can tell because the voice goes up in register and the eye contact gets intense. The shot stops feeling real.

RescueJames says "tell it to me like I'm your sister and we're having a cup of tea." Shoot one more take immediately. That take is usually the keeper.
Audio has a weird low hum on playback

Almost always the fridge compressor kicking in. Sometimes the boiler. Rarely a phone-interference buzz.

RescueUnplug the fridge for 10 minutes (plan around this). If boiler, turn off heating. If hum remains, move Kaja 2m from any appliance and re-test.
Exposure hunts during the take

The image gets darker then lighter as Kaja moves her head or someone walks past a window.

RescueBefore rolling, long-press on Kaja's face in the camera app until "AE/AF LOCK" appears at the top. Exposure is now locked.
The hero line lands flat in every take

Kaja has read the line too many times and lost the meaning.

RescueStop. Two-minute break. Kaja does one silent read, one out-loud read in a totally different tone (sarcastic, or sung), then the real take. Pattern-break fixes dead delivery.
James has visible drift on a 30-second take

Phone starts at chest, ends at navel. Very common in long takes.

RescueBetween takes, James rests the phone on a countertop edge at the correct height and checks his arm angle. Then resumes. For extra help, James can lean his back against a wall during the take.
Fridge B-roll looks staged

Too perfect. Reads like a photo shoot.

RescueKaja rearranges 2 containers to be slightly askew. Adds 1 unrelated item (a butter, a ketchup). Shoot it again. Lived-in wins.
12 · Post-production handoff

What the editor needs and what not to add

ItemNote
FilesAll .mov camera originals from iPhone 17 Pro Max, labelled C1-A-takeN, C1-B-takeN, C1-CA-1 to C1-CA-4.
Edit toolCapCut. Keep the edit simple. This does not need DaVinci.
Cut structurePick the strongest Take A as the spine. Cut to Take B on one mid-sentence beat for variety. Drop one cut-away on the "fridge" line and one on the final "eating" beat.
MusicNone until the final 2 seconds, and only if it truly helps. Silence is the default.
Text overlayOnly the final card. "Chefly. Stop thinking about dinner." Syne Extra Bold or DM Sans Bold. Cream on near-black.
CaptionsYes, burn-in. Dialogue only. Keep them small, low third, Syne Extra Bold, cream.
Length20 to 30 seconds. If you're over 32, cut harder. Every second past 30 costs view-through rate.
Exports9:16 master at 1080x1920. 1:1 square crop at 1080x1080 as a variant (for feed). Both H.264, 10 Mbps.
13 · Self-check

Quick self-check

Five questions. Try them before opening the answers.

Before you shoot, can you answer these?
Where should Kaja's eyes fall in the frame, and why?
Show answer
Answer: On the top third line of the 9:16 frame. It gives a natural sense of balance (rule of thirds), keeps small headroom above her, and means her collarbone sits roughly at the bottom edge of the chest-framed crop.
Why are we not using the Lantern 26" on this shoot?
Show answer
Answer: C1 wants a daylight feel from a single source. Adding the Lantern as a second light flattens the face and makes the shot feel studio-lit, which kills the "real customer" framing. One light, Amaran + Dome III, is the whole setup.
The DJI Mic 2 won't pair with its receiver. What do you do?
Show answer
Answer: Turn both units off, turn the RX on first, then the TX. Wait 10 seconds. If still no pair, fall back to the iPhone onboard mic, move James within 50cm of Kaja, silence the kitchen. Shot is still usable.
Which take is the editor most likely to use as the spine of the edit, and why?
Show answer
Answer: Take A (chest-framed, dead-on). It is the most intimate framing and carries the hero line best. Take B is for coverage, used to cut to on a single mid-sentence beat for variety.
Name three things that should not appear in the background.
Show answer
Answer: Dishes in the sink, competitor product logos, and Chefly-branded clothing on Kaja. Any one of these breaks the "real customer in her own kitchen" framing.
