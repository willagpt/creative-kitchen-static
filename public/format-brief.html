the one-paragraph story

Chefly ran a single creative format from 2021 to 2026: lifestyle shots with food close-ups. That format popularised the category. The imitators (Simmer, Calo UK, Frive, Your Heights) showed up later with more money and moved to UGC, founder talking-head, and offer-led static while Chefly kept running the 2021 playbook. Of the 20 recent competitor videos that have gone through ai-analyse-video v3, 50% are UGC-primary, 35% are talking-head-primary, 10% are lifestyle-primary, 5% are b-roll-heavy. The lifestyle format still appears across competitor work, but as a secondary format stacked under UGC or talking-head. Chefly is the only brand in its set running lifestyle as a standalone primary. That is the gap.

format priority, next 60 days

  1. talking-head with james on camera. highest evidence weight. simmer's 87-day winner is this format. frive and your heights both run founder-led. chefly has the actual founder story (pioneered the category in 2021) that no imitator can claim. script structure from ad 3 and ad 4.
  2. offer-led static using the d3.2 sleeve. cheapest to test, fastest to iterate, strongest for retargeting. chefly brand system already has the visual building blocks: hot yellow #FFD60A, sky blue #5CCFFF, cream sticker #FFF6EE, syne extra bold for the number, instrument serif for the support line. adapt the huel numeric-claim pattern to a chefly-specific stat.
  3. UGC with named creator or customer. higher production lift, but frive's nigel benn ad proves the playbook. chefly's customer list almost certainly has 2 to 3 people with enough audience or credibility to be the on-camera voice.
  4. lifestyle as secondary. keep producing these, but stack them as supporting cuts inside a UGC or talking-head edit (mapped to creative kitchen FOOD and LIFE clip categories).

production notes per format

talking-head

  • setting. james in a kitchen or office, warm natural light, not studio white. cream-adjacent backdrop. hand-held or tripod.
  • wardrobe. plain tonal top, no logo.
  • overlay text style. syne extra bold for hook line (2 to 4 words max), instrument serif for supporting clause. all lowercase with full stops.
  • hook line examples. "we started this in 2021." / "no chopping. no cooking. no dishes." / "one less thing to think about."
  • shot sequence (10s AIDA). 0 to 2s founder hook, 2 to 5s problem or origin story, 5 to 8s product reveal (close-up of sleeve + tray), 8 to 10s CTA card with offer.
  • creative kitchen clip mapping. new HOOK shot required (founder piece to camera), existing FOOD + PROD clips for product reveal, CTA clip exists.

offer-led static (1:1 square)

  • hero. single d3.2 sleeve in hand or on tray, shot flat or 45-degree.
  • headline placement. top-left or top-centre, syne extra bold, hot yellow #FFD60A on cream, 70 to 90px equivalent.
  • subheadline. instrument serif italic, sky blue #5CCFFF.
  • body copy. 3 to 5 bullet points with ✅ and ❌ emoji, bullet copy in primary text field on meta feed (not on image).
  • objection-first hook. "they said we're more expensive. they're right. by about £2. here's what that £2 gets you: ✅ ready in 3 minutes (not 45)…"
  • safe-zone. keep headline and CTA inside the 1:1 centre square for 4:5 and 1:1 crops.

UGC (9:16 vertical)

  • camera. shot on iphone, not studio camera. vertical. hand-held.
  • opening. first 2 seconds must be creator face-to-camera, not product.
  • caption overlay. platform-native font (instagram/tiktok default) for the first 3 to 5 seconds so it reads as organic, then switch to syne overlay for the branded CTA card at the end.
  • creator brief length. max 60 seconds of footage, cut to 15 or 20 seconds for the ad.
  • creative kitchen clip mapping. new HOOK + VAR shots required (creator content), existing FOOD clips for cutaways, existing CTA for end card.

you are fully kitted. lighting + cameras + audio + overhead rigs + neck mount + tripod are all in-house. £0 further spend needed to shoot every format in this brief. revisit kit only if a specific shot fails on the first shoot day.
gimbal note. osmo mobile 6 is a power tool, not a default. over-stabilised footage reads "brand film", which kills the UGC authenticity the simmer and megan petite coach ads trade on. keep the gimbal OFF for talking-head, chest-framed UGC, and hand-POV shots (the micro-shake is the signal). turn it ON only for (a) walk-and-talk founder shots through the kitchen, (b) a smooth dolly-feel push-in on a plated meal, (c) any product hero that wants the gliding "premium" feel as a contrast to rougher UGC cuts in the same ad.
ring-light note. the ring light on the ARKON clamp stand produces a visible circular catchlight in the subject's eyes and a hard donut reflection on anything glossy (the d3.2 cream sleeve, the tray film, plates). that reflection is the visual signature of a salesy creator-economy ad, which is exactly the register chefly is trying to step away from. default: ring light off, use the 200x + dome III instead. only turn it on for tight overhead hand-demo shots where there is no room to swing in a 200x and there are no reflective surfaces in frame.

the "filmed from the chest" look

That framing you've clocked in simmer's "one less thing" (ad 1) and megan petite coach (ad 3) is the creator-pov look, roughly a medium close-up shot sitting neck-to-chest in frame. With your current kit, two angles get you there, no extra spend:

  • handheld face-cam (free). creator holds the phone at arm's length and pulls their body close so the frame lands neck-down. reads most natural, this is the UGC look. 17 pro max front camera, portrait, 4K 30fps, stabilisation on. angle the phone slightly down toward the face so the chin doesn't get cut off. covers the first-3-seconds talking hook.
  • smallrig neck mount (owned). phone hangs MagSafe'd to the neck strap at chest height, points outward. frees both hands for product interaction: opening the sleeve, microwaving, peeling the film, plating, eating. this is the hands-POV reveal angle, not a face shot. covers the product-demo and reveal beats.
the shoot-day sequence. one ad can carry a handheld face-cam shot for the opening hook (creator talking, phone at arm's length), cut to a smallrig neck-mount POV of the product coming out of the fridge, then land on an ARKON-overhead top-down of the plated meal for the pay-off. three framings from one shoot, no camera-operator, no extra gear. every cut earns attention because the angle changes.
what about the dedicated chest harness? the simmer ad 1 and megan petite coach ad 3 look uses a chest harness (phone clamped to a chest plate pointing up at the face) for hands-free talking-while-walking. you've decided not to buy one. the workaround: use handheld face-cam for the talking beats, and the neck mount for the hands-POV beats. you lose the single shot where the creator walks through the kitchen with both hands free while talking to camera. if that turns out to be the shot you want most after shoot day one, revisit the £60 smallrig MD3189 chest mount then.

what you lose vs. a "fully-loaded" shoot

honest tradeoffs. none are dealbreakers, all have workarounds using what you already have.

  • no dedicated chest harness. you can't do the exact simmer-ad-1 walking-while-talking-hands-free shot. workaround: alternate between handheld face-cam (for talking beats) and neck mount (for hands beats). if after shoot 1 you specifically want the walking chest-mount shot, buy the smallrig MD3189 then (£60).
  • no flag panels. the cream d3.2 sleeve may have slightly softer highlights on its edge in offer-led statics. workaround: a black foamboard from ryman (£4) taped to a chair or light stand, same result.
  • no 5-in-1 reflector. workaround: use the second 200x as a dedicated fill at 15 to 25%. actually better than a reflector, just costs one more power outlet.
  • no spare sandbags. workaround: 5kg bags of rice or flour on light stand bases. already in the kitchen.
  • DJI lavaliers still missing. the DJI mic TX clipped to collar covers every audio scenario in this brief. lavs only matter for hidden-under-shirt scenarios you haven't planned yet. revisit in 2 weeks.
the answer to "am i ready to shoot?" yes. fully. the only gap worth re-evaluating after shoot day one is the chest harness, and you'll know within the first morning whether you need it.
