C3 · Hands-POV product demo

Two sequential setups, zero dialogue. Kaja's hands do all the acting. Step one: a chest-mounted POV of the kitchen pass. Step two: an overhead desk pass. The goal is 15 to 20 seconds of satisfying, tactile product demo that cuts like butter.

Priority 3 Silent · foley only 2 setups · sequential POV + overhead 15 to 20s final ~60 min on set Difficulty: medium

Learning goals

  • Rig the SmallRig Magnetic Neck Mount on Kaja's chest and frame a usable POV shot
  • Mount the ARKON Pro Stand overhead for a clean top-down desk pass
  • Light two very different setups with the same two lights, swapping position between them
  • Plan a 6-beat action sequence that the editor can cut into a tight 15s spot
  • Record foley (opening, peeling, scraping, microwave, cutlery) as the audio bed

Pre-requisites

  • C1 and C2 wrapped. Audio kit should already be packed up.
  • Read Training Companion Parts 2, 3, 4 and Part 5 §C3
  • Both iPhones at 50%+ charge. Neck mount and ARKON Pro Stand physically on set.
  • Kaja has practiced the action once without recording — get the beats into her hands

What you'll deliver

  • 3 POV passes of the 5-beat kitchen action (unbox, microwave, scrape, plate, fork)
  • 3 overhead passes of the desk pass (container placed, lid off, fork down, eating frame)
  • Clean foley for every touch and movement — no voiceover, no music yet
  • One macro beat of steam rising as the lid comes off (this sells the whole thing)

01 · Concept

What we're making and why it works

No face. No voice. Just hands doing the work. The product is the star.

C3 is the hands-POV product demo: a silent, texture-heavy 15 to 20 second showpiece that answers the unspoken question "what does using this actually look like?" Every cut is an action. Every sound is a touch or a microwave beep. The whole ad lives on foley and motion.

We're shooting it in two sequential setups, not simultaneous. First setup is a chest-mounted POV in the kitchen, with Kaja's hands opening the box, loading the microwave, and lifting the lid. Second setup is an overhead desk pass where her hands place the heated container down, peel it back, and pick up a fork. Same container, same meal, two totally different visual languages that cut together beautifully.

Plain-english summaryKaja puts on a chest mount and films her own hands cooking. Then we put an iPhone above the desk and film her hands eating. No talking. Just motion and sound. The editor lays a beat under it.

Reference winners this pattern came from

  • Simmer ad 3 — chest-mounted POV of unboxing and microwave. Scored top-quintile for shot variety and completion rate.
  • Huel morning routine ad — overhead desk pass with a single ring light. Zero voiceover. Foley + beat did all the work.
  • Both ads succeeded because the viewer unconsciously imagines themselves doing it. POV is the most first-person format on social.

04 · Lighting

Lighting, step by step

Small ring light does the hero work. Amaran is background wash. Two lights, two positions between setups.

Setup 1 · kitchen POV
Ring light above-right, lighting the container. Amaran lights the back wall for depth and colour. Kaja stands at the counter with the chest mount on.
Setup 2 · overhead desk
iPhone overhead on ARKON Pro Stand. Ring light above-right of the desk, pointed down at the container. Amaran camera-left as soft ambient.

Step by step · setup 1 (POV kitchen)

  1. Put the neck mount on Kaja first. Adjust strap so the iPhone sits horizontal at mid-sternum height, roughly 20cm below her chin. Lock the ball joint once level.
  2. Pre-run the action. Kaja opens the container, puts it in the microwave, closes the door, presses start. James watches the live preview on a second phone over AirDrop Mirror (if unavailable, watch the iPhone's flipped-selfie screen when possible). Correct any head-tilt so the frame stays level.
  3. Rig the ring light. Clamp to counter edge camera-right (from Kaja's POV, upper-right of her frame). Angle down 45°, 5600K, 40% brightness.
  4. Set the Amaran + Dome III aimed at the back wall, 2m from Kaja, 5600K, 30%. No direct shot at Kaja.
  5. Kill overhead kitchen lights. Always.
  6. One dry run. Kaja performs the full 5-beat action silently, start to finish, without rolling. This trains her hands so the real take is fluid.

Step by step · setup 2 (overhead desk)

  1. Take the iPhone off the neck mount. Remove the mount. Take a 30-second break for Kaja.
  2. Set up the ARKON Pro Stand next to the desk. Extend the arm so the iPhone sits centred above the desk, roughly 70 to 90cm above the container. Phone in portrait (not landscape) pointing straight down.
  3. Preview and level the camera. The container should be dead-centre. Any tilt and the shot looks wrong. Most ARKON arms have a small bubble or you can eyeball a straight line against the desk edge.
  4. Move the ring light to camera-right of the desk, above and slightly forward. 5600K, 40%. Hot-spot check: move it until reflection on the container lid is soft, not mirror.
  5. Move the Amaran to camera-left of the desk, 2m back, pointed at the area beyond the container (not at it). 30%.
  6. Lock focus and exposure on the container in the iPhone camera preview before rolling.
Rule of thumbIf the ring light is reflected as a bright ring on the container lid, it's too close or too direct. Move it back 20cm and angle slightly off-axis until the reflection disappears.
05 · Audio

Foley-only audio

Every sound the viewer hears comes from the action. No voice. No music (yet). The phone's onboard mic is doing all the work.

  1. No DJI mic on this shoot. Pack it away. The chest mount pressed against the TX box creates body rumble.
  2. Kitchen must be silent except for the action. Fridge unplugged for 10 minutes. Boiler off. Any fan off.
  3. James cannot speak during any take. Not even a whispered "okay". The onboard mic will catch him.
  4. Kaja's feet matter. Socks only, no hard-soled shoes. A footfall across a wooden floor will wreck the take.
  5. Deliberately sound the action. The click of the microwave button. The plastic peel of the lid. The soft thud of the fork going down. Let them happen cleanly, slightly slower than real life.
  6. Between each action beat, hold for 1 second. Gives the editor a clean gap to trim to.
  7. Playback check on AirPods after every take. Listen for breath, clothing rustle, or anything that isn't the action. If you hear it, re-take.
Back-up planIf the kitchen cannot be made silent (flatmate, street noise), record the action silently and add foley in post from free libraries. Keep the real foley as a reference track for timing.
06 · Hands, wardrobe and set

Hands, wardrobe and set dressing

Kaja's hands are the face of this ad. Everything they touch needs to look clean and real.

Do
  • Clean, trimmed nails. No polish, or plain single-colour only (nude, cream).
  • Hands recently moisturised — dry skin on camera looks dusty
  • Sleeves that end at wrist or are pushed to forearm. Plain colour, no cuffs.
  • A real wooden cutting board, not a stylised prop board
  • A clean, dry, neutral-colour tea towel placed off-frame but within reach
  • Utensils are normal kitchen cutlery, not stylised food-photography silverware
  • Dark or neutral desk surface for the overhead pass (contrast with the container)
Don't
  • Chipped polish, paint, food stains, or plasters
  • Loose bracelets, rings on every finger, or a watch with a bright face
  • Baggy sleeves that swing into frame
  • Shiny, reflective, or patterned desk surface that reflects the ring light
  • Wet hands that leave water marks on the container or desk
  • A fork with food residue already on it — pre-clean everything
  • Paper towel, kitchen roll, or plastic wrap visible in the frame
07 · Beat sheet

The beat sheet, no script

There is no dialogue. There are beats. Kaja performs the same beats in both setups, different angles.

Beat 1
Hands enter frame. Container placed on surface. Slow placement. Both hands holding the container. Don't slide it, set it down. The thud is usable.
Beat 2
Lid peeled back. One single peel motion, diagonal from one corner. Hold the lid up at the end so it's readable in frame for half a second.
Beat 3
Container into microwave, door closed, button pressed, door reopened. SETUP 1 ONLY. Do each sub-action with a full visible beat between them. Don't rush. The button-press is the audio payoff.
Beat 4
Lid lifted. Steam rises. This is the money beat of the ad. Hold the lid up for a full 2 seconds. Steam needs a clean dark background to read. Pause before the next beat.
Beat 5
Fork picked up, one cut into the food, one bite prep. Close-up on the food surface. If there's a glossy sauce, angle the fork so it catches the ring light briefly.
Beat 6
Fork comes down. Frame holds empty for 1 second. SETUP 2 ONLY. Final frame is the empty desk with the fork resting on the container edge. This is the landing for the end card in post.
End
Text overlay in post: "90 seconds. No thinking." Added in CapCut. Syne Extra Bold. Cream on near-black. Fades in on beat 6's held frame.

09 · Timing plan

Minute-by-minute on the day

60 minutes from "ready to start C3" to wrap. Setup 1 to setup 2 swap-over is the hardest moment, plan for it.

min 0 to 10Setup 1 rig
Fit neck mount on Kaja, level the iPhone. Clamp ring light to counter camera-right. Position Amaran on back wall. Silence kitchen — unplug fridge, boiler off. Dry-run the 5-beat action once without recording.
min 10 to 25Setup 1 shoot
Shoot shots 1 to 3. Three takes per shot. Playback check between each for head-tilt, audio, and focus. The lid-lift macro (shot 3) gets an extra back-up take — it's the hero beat.
min 25 to 35Swap to setup 2
Remove neck mount. Kaja breaks for water. James dismantles ring light from kitchen clamp, rigs ARKON Pro Stand overhead at the desk. Moves Amaran to desk-left. Re-clamps ring light to desk-side. Levels the overhead phone. Pre-rolls and confirms the desk is centred and the container spot is marked.
min 35 to 50Setup 2 shoot
Shoot shots 4 to 6. Three takes per shot. Playback check for hand-edge symmetry and rest-frame consistency. Reheat the container between takes if needed — only beat 4 onwards uses "hot" food.
min 50 to 60Review + wrap
AirDrop all files off the iPhone. Label C3-POV-N, C3-OH-N (where N is the shot number). Confirm audio is clean. Confirm the steam beat is in the can. Take down lights, pack kit.
10 · Quality gates

You're done when...

Do not wrap until all of these are ticked.

C3 sign-off checklist
  • 3 usable POV takes for each of shots 1, 2, 3
  • 3 usable overhead takes for each of shots 4, 5, 6
  • Steam is visibly readable on at least one take of shot 3 (the lid-lift macro)
  • Container lands on the same chalked spot for all overhead takes
  • No voiceovers, footsteps, fridge hum, or external noise audible on playback
  • Kaja's hands are clean, moisturised, and identical across all takes (no visible food smudge between takes)
  • Files labelled C3-POV-1 to C3-POV-3 and C3-OH-4 to C3-OH-6, AirDropped off the phone
  • Overhead iPhone is confirmed level — container is a clean rectangle, not a trapezoid, in the final footage
11 · Common mistakes + rescues

What usually goes wrong and how to fix it fast

Neck mount tilts as Kaja moves

Frame drifts from level to leaning across the take. Especially on longer actions like the microwave loading.

RescueRe-level the ball joint between every take, not every shot. Kaja consciously holds her chest square and moves only her arms, not her torso. If it persists, strap the mount tighter and add a shim of folded cloth under the base.
Steam doesn't read on camera

You can see it in the room but it disappears in the frame.

RescueThree fixes: (1) place a dark cloth/board behind the container so steam has contrast, (2) wait until the meal is piping hot — re-microwave for 30s if needed, (3) lower the ring light slightly so it backlights the steam rather than washing it out.
Overhead iPhone is not truly level

Container looks like a trapezoid in the frame. Editor can see the skew immediately.

RescueBefore rolling, place a square piece of paper on the desk and check that all 4 edges are parallel to the iPhone screen edges in preview. Adjust ARKON ball joint until they are. Most ARKON heads have a locking dial — tighten it after levelling.
Hot reflection of the ring light on the container lid

A bright circular highlight dominates the frame. Un-fixable in post without cropping.

RescueMove the ring light further away (pull back 30cm) and angle it off-axis by 15°. Test in preview. The reflection should soften into a diffuse glow, not a mirror.
Audio picks up breathing or fabric rustle

A low "ch-ch" or "hhh" sound under the foley. Wrecks the silence.

RescueNeck mount sits too high against Kaja's shirt collar. Lower the strap by a few cm so the iPhone isn't touching fabric. Kaja breathes through her nose between beats, not her mouth.
Fork rest-frame angle is different on every take

Editor can't match cuts at the end of the ad.

RescuePut a small dot of masking tape on the desk where the fork handle should rest. Kaja drops the fork to the mark every time. Removes guesswork.
12 · Post-production handoff

What the editor needs and what not to add

ItemNote
FilesCamera originals .mov. Labelled C3-POV-1 to C3-POV-3 (shots 1 to 3) and C3-OH-4 to C3-OH-6 (shots 4 to 6). Multiple takes named -takeN.
Edit toolCapCut. Simple cut-and-foley edit.
Cut structurePOV → overhead → POV steam macro → overhead lid peel → overhead bite → rest frame for end card. Each beat 2 to 3 seconds. Total 15 to 20 seconds.
Hero beatShot 3 (lid lift + steam). Slow this to 75% for 2 seconds. Only slow it once. If you slow everything the ad feels stoned.
MusicLow-BPM organic beat bed (licensed or royalty-free). Comes in at 0:03. Fades out on the rest frame.
FoleyKeep all on-set foley. Augment with free foley (microwave beep, fork-on-ceramic) only if on-set audio has a dropout.
Text overlayOnly "90 seconds. No thinking." on the rest frame. Syne Extra Bold. Cream on near-black. No other text anywhere.
CaptionsNone. There is no dialogue. Do not add explainer captions.
Length15 to 20 seconds. Not a second longer. This is a quick-hit format.
Exports9:16 master 1080x1920 H.264 10 Mbps. 1:1 square 1080x1080 variant for feed.
13 · Self-check

Quick self-check

Five questions. Try them before opening the answers.

Before you shoot, can you answer these?
Why don't we use the DJI Mic 2 on this shoot?
Show answer
Answer: The chest mount presses the TX box against Kaja's body and introduces rumble, fabric rustle, and breath. The onboard iPhone mic, combined with a silent kitchen, produces cleaner foley for this format.
Which shot is the "hero beat" and why?
Show answer
Answer: Shot 3, the POV lid-lift with steam rising. It sells "hot, ready, real food" in one silent visual and is the moment the ad emotionally pivots from process to payoff. The editor will slow this one beat to 75% for impact.
How do you know the overhead iPhone is truly level before rolling shot 4?
Show answer
Answer: Place a square piece of paper on the desk. Its four edges should be parallel to the iPhone screen edges in preview. If they're not, adjust the ARKON ball joint until they are and lock it. A trapezoid frame is unfixable in post.
Why is the container placement marked with chalk on the desk?
Show answer
Answer: So the container lands in the same spot on every take. If it drifts, the editor can't match-cut between takes and the cut feels like a mistake. Same reason the fork rest-position gets a masking-tape mark.
Steam isn't reading on camera in shot 3. Give two fixes.
Show answer
Answer: (1) Place a dark cloth or board behind the container so steam has contrast to rise against. (2) Re-heat the meal until piping hot — steam needs real heat. Third option: lower the ring light slightly so it backlights the steam instead of washing it.
