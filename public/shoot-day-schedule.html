Shoot day schedule + cadence

Two honest schedules (day 1 first-timer vs day 3+ muscle memory), two split-day alternatives for lower cognitive load, a night-before prep checklist, and a monthly rhythm that keeps Meta fed without burning out either of you.

Planning doc C1 to C5 sequenced Day 1: ~9 hrs Day 3+: ~6 hrs Weekly + bi-weekly cadence ~50 creatives/month output

The headline answer

  • Day 1: budget 9 hours, plan for a full day with a proper lunch
  • Day 3+: 6 hours end-to-end, genuinely half a day
  • Better path for first timers: split C1 + C2 from C3 + C4 + C5 across 2 half-days one week apart
  • Steady-state rhythm: 1 full shoot day every 2 weeks + 1 weekly 90-min C2 reaction shoot

Why the 4 hr 15 min figure is misleading

  • The 45-min blocks in each pack count on-the-clock filming only, not setup
  • Transitions between C1 and C2 alone eat 20 to 30 min (kit change, re-blocking)
  • Wardrobe changes between Kaja-on-camera and James-on-camera take 15 to 20 min
  • First-time learning tax is 50% on top of every block for shoot day 1
  • Charging, lunch, debrief and file transfer aren't in the 4:15 figure

What good looks like

  • Kit is charged and sets are dressed the night before, not the morning of
  • Transitions between concepts are under 15 min because the next setup is pre-prepped
  • No-one feels rushed on takes because buffer time is real
  • Files on Drive and labelled within 24 hours of wrap, every single time

01 · Cadence

How often should we shoot?

A three-tier rhythm that matches your stage (pre-1,000 weekly customers, testing not scaling), beats Meta creative fatigue, and keeps both of you fresh.

Meta creative fatigue typically kicks in at 7 to 14 days on a single creative. That means your ad account needs roughly 2 to 3 fresh creatives entering the rotation every week to keep CPAs stable. The rhythm below produces ~50 new creatives per month, enough to rotate 2 to 3 fresh ads into Meta every week indefinitely.

Short versionOne 6-hour shoot day every other Wednesday, one 90-minute founder-head shoot every Friday, one 2-hour creative review the first Thursday of every month. No Monday or Tuesday filming. That's it.
Tier 1 · core rhythm

Weekly C2 reaction shoot

Every Friday · 90 min · James on camera

Fixed slot, same setup every week, low stress. Kaja stays off-camera, runs audio monitor and operates the second iPhone. Film 3 to 5 founder talking-head clips reacting to that week's news, trends, customer reviews, or offers.

  • No wardrobe change (James wears Chefly navy crewneck every week)
  • Same desk, same 2 cameras, same DJI mic, same lighting
  • Script comes from Wednesday's Slack notes and that week's Meta data
  • Done in 90 min because there's no decision-making, just execute
Output · 3 to 5 C2 creatives per week · ~15 Meta exports after aspect resize
Tier 2 · volume rhythm

Bi-weekly full shoot day

Every other Wednesday · 6 hrs · both of you

The big batch. Run C1 to C5 back-to-back using the Day 3+ schedule below. This is where the testimonial, the hands-POV demo, the offer statics, and the overhead hero film come from. High output, high variety, only twice a month.

  • Full kit setup, full wardrobe prep, full lunch break
  • Output is 15 to 20 ad-ready creatives per session, mixed across 5 formats
  • Wednesday works because food is freshest mid-week and Thursday is left clear for edit + approval before Friday's C2
  • Skip C1 (testimonial) on alternate sessions if Kaja is tired; re-run only C2+C3+C4+C5
Output · 15 to 20 mixed creatives per session · ~50 Meta exports after aspect resize
Tier 3 · brain rhythm

Monthly creative review

First Thursday of month · 2 hrs · no camera

Desk work only. Review the last month's Meta performance data using the ad-kill-keep skill. Kill fatigued creatives. Approve the next 10 to 15 to enter rotation. Plan scripts and offers for the next two bi-weekly shoots.

  • No shooting, no filming, no kit prep
  • Output is a prioritised script list and an offer calendar for the coming 4 weeks
  • This is where creative strategy gets decided, not on shoot day
  • Skipping this is how shoot days drift into "film whatever" territory

02 · Output

What this rhythm yields every month

The maths behind the cadence so you can see why it's enough to beat creative fatigue.

Why 50 is enoughMeta's rule of thumb is 2 to 3 fresh creatives per week to beat fatigue on a £30 to £100/day account. That's 8 to 12 per month. You'll produce ~50, so you can afford to be picky: only the top half go live, the bottom half stay in the library for later A/B tests.
03 · Night-before

Prep checklist (do this the evening before)

The single biggest determinant of whether shoot day runs on schedule is how much you prep the night before. 30 minutes of prep saves 2 hours of set time.

Kit: charging
  • iPhone 17 Pro Max charged to 100%, off the cable by bedtime
  • Backup iPhone (or Kaja's phone) charged to 100% for C2 second-camera angle
  • Amaran 200x battery fully charged (overnight on charger)
  • Amaran Dome III and Lantern 26" ready to go (mains-powered, no battery needed)
  • DJI Mic 2 TX + RX charged (they share a case, just leave on the mat)
  • ARKON Pro Stand clamp tightened, arm tested (swing it through the overhead arc once)
  • SmallRig Magnetic Neck Mount strap adjusted, magnet test done
  • Manfrotto Compact Tripod legs clean, ball head locked
Set dressing: kitchen
  • Counter wiped and crumb-free, 2 to 3 branded containers staged on the shelf behind
  • Fridge stocked with 4 to 6 Chefly containers, labels forward
  • Backdrop checked (wall free of distracting posters, plants arranged)
  • Mic placement marked with a faint pencil dot on Kaja's usual collar position
Set dressing: desk
  • Desk cleared, oak chopping board ready, ivory linen runner folded nearby
  • Ramekin with parsley sprig wrapped in damp kitchen roll, in fridge
  • Fork and spoon polished with a dry cloth, fingerprints off
  • Amaran 200x and Lantern positions marked on the floor with gaffer tape
  • Black A4 card (flag for C4) in the props box, desk-side
  • X-Rite ColorChecker in its protective sleeve, desk-side
Wardrobe + talent
  • Kaja's outfit for C1 hung, ironed, hair plan agreed (if a change is needed for C3)
  • James's Chefly navy crewneck washed, ironed, on a hanger
  • Both have drunk water, had dinner, are not hungover
  • Both have read the pack for whichever concept starts day one
Food
  • Hero meal (the one you'll feature in C4 and C5) defrosted in fridge, ready for morning
  • One backup portion in the freezer in case of a reheat mistake
  • Microwave cleaned, plate inside swapped for a clean one
If you only do one thing the night beforeCharge the batteries and dress the kitchen set. Everything else can be rescued in the morning. Dead batteries and a messy counter will cost you the full day.
04 · Day 1 schedule

First-timer schedule (~9 hours)

The first time you run C1 to C5 back-to-back. Builds in a 50% learning-tax buffer on each block, a proper 45-minute lunch, and realistic transitions. Assumes you arrived with kit charged and sets dressed.

Day 1 · First full run of C1 to C5

08:00 to 16:55 · total 8 hrs 55 min
08:00 30 min Arrival + kit check. Final battery check, set-dressing audit, wardrobe on, Kaja hair/makeup touch-up. Walk through C1 pack once.You're not rolling yet. Don't rush this.
08:30 70 min C1 · Kaja chest-framed testimonial (nominal 45 min + 25 min learning buffer). Handheld, one iPhone, 2 angles, 3 takes per angle, 4 silent cut-aways.Kaja on camera. James shooter. Script in Kaja's earshot on a second phone.
09:40 20 min Transition to C2. Kaja off-camera, James changes into Chefly navy crewneck. Strike handheld kit, set up 2 tripods, pair second iPhone, rig DJI mic on James, lighting shift for desk.
10:00 90 min C2 · James founder talking head (nominal 60 min + 30 min learning buffer). 2 cameras rolling simultaneously, script on iPad, 3 message variants per take.James on camera. Kaja runs audio monitor and time-codes.
11:30 45 min Lunch. Real lunch, off-set, away from the kit. Eat something warm, hydrate, review morning takes on the phone.Do not skip on day 1. Everyone will underperform after 3 hours without food.
12:15 15 min Transition to C3. Strip C2 desk kit, attach SmallRig Magnetic Neck Mount to Kaja, walk through to the kitchen, confirm neck-mount stability with the lean test.
12:30 90 min C3 · Hands-POV product demo (nominal 60 min + 30 min learning buffer). Part A kitchen POV (neck mount), Part B overhead desk (ARKON). Two locations, two rigs, one run.Kaja's hands only. No dialogue. Edit-friendly continuous takes.
14:00 15 min Transition to C4. ARKON stays. Swap iPhone from overhead to Manfrotto tripod, reposition to 25 degrees top-down, Lantern moves camera-right, black A4 flag in place, X-Rite card ready.
14:15 60 min C4 · Offer-led static frame (nominal 45 min + 15 min learning buffer). ProRAW stills of 3 variants, plus one 3-second push-in video take.No talent. Both of you work the set and the camera together.
15:15 10 min Transition to C5. Same desk, same two lights. Re-rig the iPhone to the ARKON Pro Stand overhead arm at 45 cm height, offset the key 60 degrees behind camera. Microwave the hero meal on standby.
15:25 60 min C5 · Top-down food hero (nominal 45 min + 15 min learning buffer). 3 video takes of the 10-second 5-beat sequence (reheat between takes), 3 ProRAW stills from the same rig.Kaja's hands plate and serve. James calls the beats.
16:25 30 min Wrap + Drive upload + debrief. Kit strike, files copied to Drive with clear naming (C1_kaja_testimonial_angle1_take3.mov), both of you sit down, note one thing that worked and one that didn't for next time.
Total elapsed 8 hrs 55 min · full day
Day 1 verdictPlan this as a full working day. Don't book anything for the evening. Don't promise yourself you'll edit tonight. You'll be tired. Just back up the files, lay out tomorrow's clothes, go to bed.
05 · Day 3+ schedule

Muscle-memory schedule (~6 hours)

Once you've shot C1 to C5 two or three times, the learning tax drops to zero and you can compress transitions. This is the "just over half a day" outcome.

Day 3+ · Muscle-memory run

09:00 to 14:45 · total 5 hrs 45 min
08:30 30 min Kit + wardrobe check. Batteries, Drive folder created, hair done, Kaja outfit on. Pre-shoot prep done the night before, so this is just a sanity pass.
09:00 45 min C1 · Kaja chest-framed testimonial. Running to the nominal pack time. 2 angles, 3 takes each, 4 cut-aways. No learning buffer needed.
09:45 15 min Transition to C2. James changes, 2 tripods go up, DJI mic rigged. You know the order.
10:00 60 min C2 · James founder talking head. 2 cameras, script on iPad, 3 message variants.
11:00 10 min Transition to C3. Neck mount on Kaja, lean test, move to kitchen.
11:10 60 min C3 · Hands-POV demo. Kitchen POV + overhead desk. Flow is familiar, no re-explanation needed.
12:10 30 min Lunch. Short but real. Off-set, hydrate, glance at takes.
12:40 10 min Transition to C4. Tripod reposition, Lantern camera-right, flag in, X-Rite card ready.
12:50 45 min C4 · Offer-led static. 3 variants ProRAW + one push-in. Both of you work the set together.
13:35 5 min Transition to C5. Just the ARKON re-rig and the key light offset to 60 degrees behind. Micro-transition.
13:40 45 min C5 · Top-down food hero. 3 video takes, 3 stills. Reheat between takes for steam.
14:25 20 min Wrap + Drive upload. Files copied, labels applied, one-line debrief.
Total elapsed 6 hrs 15 min · just over half a day
Day 3+ verdictYou're done by mid-afternoon. The rest of the day is yours. Edit tonight if you want, or leave it for tomorrow. This is the sustainable cadence you're aiming for.
06 · Split-day A

Alternative A: dialogue vs silent split

Two half-days, one week apart. Best for first timers, because you separate the high-performance concepts (talking on camera) from the production concepts (no-one talking).

Half-day A · morning · ~4 hrs

Dialogue shoot: C1 + C2

Both concepts need scripts, performance, audio, multiple takes. Keep them together. You'll be mentally fried by lunch; that's fine, the second half-day is physical not verbal.

  • 09:00 to 10:10 · C1 Kaja testimonial (70 min)
  • 10:10 to 10:30 · Transition (20 min)
  • 10:30 to 12:00 · C2 James founder head (90 min)
  • 12:00 to 12:30 · Wrap, debrief, file backup
Half-day B · morning, ~1 week later · ~4 hrs

Silent production: C3 + C4 + C5

No scripts, no performance, no wardrobe changes. All three concepts share the desk, so transitions are fast. You can knock out 10 to 14 creatives in a morning.

  • 09:00 to 10:30 · C3 Hands-POV (90 min incl. transition)
  • 10:30 to 11:30 · C4 Offer static (60 min)
  • 11:30 to 12:30 · C5 Top-down hero (60 min)
  • 12:30 to 13:00 · Wrap, debrief, file backup
When to choose Split AYour first ever shoot. Or any shoot where Kaja is mentally tired and you don't want her to have to hold a script in her head for 6 straight hours. The gap between the two half-days lets you review the A footage before committing to B.
07 · Split-day B

Alternative B: kitchen vs desk split

Same day, just organised by location. Cuts the kitchen→desk transition overhead by running all kitchen concepts first.

Block A · morning · ~3 hrs

Kitchen block: C1 + C3 Part A

Both shot in the same kitchen space. One set dress, one lighting setup (natural daylight for C3 POV, add Amaran for C1). No location change mid-block.

  • 09:00 to 10:00 · C1 Kaja testimonial (60 min)
  • 10:00 to 10:15 · Transition: neck mount on Kaja
  • 10:15 to 11:00 · C3 Part A kitchen POV (45 min)
  • 11:00 to 12:00 · Lunch + desk set-up
Block B · afternoon · ~3 hrs

Desk block: C2 + C3 Part B + C4 + C5

All four concepts use the same desk. Lights stay up. Only the camera rig changes per concept. Fastest possible sequencing.

  • 12:00 to 13:15 · C2 James founder head (75 min)
  • 13:15 to 13:45 · C3 Part B overhead desk (30 min)
  • 13:45 to 14:30 · C4 Offer static (45 min)
  • 14:30 to 15:15 · C5 Top-down hero (45 min)
When to choose Split BYou've done the shoots before and want the fastest possible full-day run. Total elapsed is ~6 hours 15 min (same as Day 3+), but transitions are 40% shorter because you eliminate the back-and-forth between kitchen and desk.
08 · Weekly C2

The weekly 90-minute C2 reaction shoot

Fixed slot every Friday morning. Same setup every single time. This is the engine of your weekly Meta rotation.

Weekly · Friday · 09:30 to 11:00

90 min total · James on camera · Kaja monitoring
09:30 15 min Setup. Pull tripods from storage, set up in the same marked positions. DJI mic on James. Script list open on iPad. Desk cleared.
09:45 60 min Shoot. 3 to 5 script variants. Each variant: 2 cameras, 2 takes. Script changes weekly based on: (a) Wednesday's Meta performance review, (b) that week's customer reviews, (c) current offers, (d) any news or trends.
10:45 15 min Wrap + Drive. Files copied and labelled. No edit today. Hand off to editor or queue for Saturday editing block.
Why this worksThe decisions are all made before you walk in: which offer to feature, which script to run, which angle. The shoot itself is execution. 60 minutes is plenty when you know exactly what you're saying.
09 · Monthly review

Monthly creative review (no camera, 2 hours)

First Thursday of every month. Desk work only, no Monday or Tuesday touch. This is where the strategy for the next 4 weeks gets set. Skipping this is how shoot days drift into "film whatever, we'll figure it out in the edit" territory.

Agenda (in order)

  1. Meta performance review (30 min). Run the ad-kill-keep skill on every live creative. Red = kill. Amber = watch. Green = keep. Output: a kill list and a keep list.
  2. Winning patterns (20 min). What did the top 3 creatives have in common? Hook type? Format? CTA? Write 2 sentences on the pattern.
  3. Losing patterns (10 min). What did the bottom 3 have in common? Same question, 2 sentences.
  4. Next 2 shoot plans (45 min). Decide the scripts, offers, and creative angles for the next 2 bi-weekly shoots. Write them down in shared Notes.
  5. Weekly C2 slate (15 min). List the 4 Friday scripts for the next month: what offer, what script beat, what hook.

Outputs

  • Kill list (creatives to pause in Meta Ads Manager today)
  • Keep list (creatives that stay live, with a note on what to watch for)
  • Next 2 bi-weekly shoot plans (locked scripts, locked offers)
  • Next 4 Friday C2 scripts (locked topics)
Where this goesSave the outputs to a shared Notes doc called "Creative plan · [Month Year]". Reference it at every weekly shoot and every bi-weekly shoot. No script-by-the-seat-of-your-pants. You'll thank yourself on Friday morning when you can just walk in and shoot.
10 · Red flags

When to call the shoot (don't grind through a bad day)

You'll save more money stopping early than pushing through. Bad takes are unusable and you'll know within the first 30 minutes whether the day is on or off.

Red flag 1: Kaja or James is sick, tired, or stressed

Performance concepts (C1, C2) amplify whatever energy is in the room. If either of you is running at 60%, the footage will look 60%. Reschedule to next week. The weekly C2 buffer means one missed week isn't catastrophic.

Red flag 2: Technical issue by minute 30

If the iPhone won't focus-lock, the DJI mic keeps cutting out, or a light won't fire by 30 minutes into setup, stop. Troubleshoot, if unresolved in another 30 minutes, call it. Most technical issues are battery-adjacent. Swap and retry. If still broken after 45 min total, you've lost the morning, push to afternoon or tomorrow.

Red flag 3: The food looks wrong

If the hero meal doesn't look appetising (colour off, portion wrong, steam not rising), do not shoot it. C4 and C5 are zero-salvage if the food is wrong in frame. Reheat, re-plate, rehearse off-camera, restart the block. Budget 15 min for a food reset; if it takes more than that, reschedule C4+C5 to the next shoot.

Red flag 4: You can't agree on the script

C2 is a talking-head, performance is everything. If James and Kaja start arguing about the script at 10:05, the take at 10:30 will be flat. Stop. Have the disagreement over lunch. Come back with one version. Don't film conflict into the frame.

Red flag 5: You're 90 minutes behind schedule by concept 3

If you hit C3 and you're 90+ min behind, drop C4 or C5 (not both). C5 is the biggest production lift, so dropping that first. Pushing all 5 concepts into a 10-hour day means the last 2 concepts will be rushed and weak. Better to ship 3 strong concepts than 5 mediocre ones.
